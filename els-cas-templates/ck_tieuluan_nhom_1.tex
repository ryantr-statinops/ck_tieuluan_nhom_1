\begin{abstract}
	In this paper, we consider the linearized backward Euler ﬁnite element scheme for the Navier-Stokes-Poisson-Nernst-Planck (NSPNP) equations. We aim to derive the unconditional error estimates of all variables in $l^\infty(L^2)$ and $l^\infty(H^1)$ norms. Compared with some previous results, we remove the mesh restriction. In addition, we derive two important physical properties for the proposed numerical scheme. Finally, three numerical examples are implemented to verify the theoretical analysis.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The NSPNP equations describe the dynamics of electrically charged fluids, the motions of ionized particles or molecules and their interactions with electric fields and the surrounding fluid, which are widely used in colloidal science, biophysics, semiconductors, micro- and nanofluidics, and electrochemistry \cite{Markowich1990,Kirby2010,Ramos2011,Gubbiotti2022}. The NSPNP equations are given as follows

\begin{equation}
\left\{
\begin{array}{l}
u_t - \Delta u + (u \cdot \nabla)u + \nabla p = -(c_1 - c_2)\nabla\psi, \ \text{in } \Omega \times (0,T], \\[4pt]
\text{div}\, u = 0, \ \text{in } \Omega \times (0,T], \\[4pt]
c_{1t} - \Delta c_1 - \text{div}\,(c_1\nabla\psi) + (u \cdot \nabla)c_1 = 0, \ \text{in } \Omega \times (0,T], \\[4pt]
c_{2t} - \Delta c_2 + \text{div}\,(c_2\nabla\psi) + (u \cdot \nabla)c_2 = 0, \ \text{in } \Omega \times (0,T], \\[4pt]
-\Delta\psi = c_1 - c_2, \ \text{in } \Omega \times (0,T], \\[4pt]
u = 0, \ \dfrac{\partial c_1}{\partial n} = \dfrac{\partial c_2}{\partial n} = \dfrac{\partial \psi}{\partial n} = 0, \ \text{on } \partial\Omega \times (0,T], \\[4pt]
c_1(x,0) = c_{10}(x),\ c_2(x,0) = c_{20}(x),\ u(x,0) = u_0(x),
\end{array}
\right.
\label{eq:1.1}
\end{equation}

where $\Omega \in \mathcal{R}^d$, $d = 2, 3$ is a bounded domain with a Lipschitz continuous boundary $\partial\Omega$, $n$ is the outward unit-normal vector to $\partial\Omega$, $c_1$ and $c_2$ are the concentrations of positive and negative charges, $u$ and $p$ are the velocity and pressure of the fluid, and $\psi$ is the quasi-electrostatic potential. For the qualitative analysis of the solutions, such as the existence, uniqueness, stability and asymptotic behavior, we refer to \cite{Schmuck2009,Jerome2009,Fischer2017,Bothe2014,Constantin2021,Constantin2019,Gong2021}.

Many numerical methods have been developed for the PNP and NSPNP equations. It is crucial to preserve some important physical properties at the discrete level, such as the mass conservation, concentration positivity and energy stability, and the related work can be referred to \cite{Flavell2014,He2019,Liu2021,Liu22021,Ding2020,Metti2016,Fu2022,Prohl2009,Prohl2010,Su2022,Liu2017,Shen2021,Huang2021,Gharibi2021,Dehghan2022,Gharibi22021}. In addition, some numerical techniques and implementation strategies were proposed to improve the performance efficiency, such as the adaptive finite element methods \cite{Hao2022,Yang2022,Zhu2022}, the two grid methods \cite{Shen22020}, the stabilized techniques \cite{Wang2021,Li2022}, the proper orthogonal decomposition method \cite{Dehghan22022}, and some improved iteration techniques \cite{Ying2021,Xie2020,Liu32022,Bousquet2018}.

There are some studies about the error estimates of the finite element method for the PNP and NSPNP equations. Sun and He analyzed an implicit Crank-Nicolson finite element scheme for the PNP equations in \cite{Sun2016}, and obtained the optimal error estimates in $l^\infty(H^1)$ norm for all variables, however, the convergence order of $l^\infty(L^2)$ norm was suboptimal. They also proposed the mixed finite element method for the PNP equations \cite{He22017}, and obtained optimal error estimates for $l^\infty(H^1)$ and $l^\infty(L^2)$ norms, and later they discussed the PNP-Stokes equations and modified NSPNP equations \cite{He32018,He42021}. Shi and Yang et al. investigated the conforming and nonconforming finite element methods on rectangular meshes \cite{Shi2019,Shi22021}, and obtained the superconvergence results by integral identities techniques. Yang et al. researched the virtual element method on general polygonal meshes \cite{Yang22022}, and derived the error estimates in $l^\infty(H^1)$ and $l^\infty(L^2)$ norms. However, due to the use of inverse inequality and mathematical inductive argument, a mesh restriction is needed in the above references \cite{Sun2016,He22017,He32018,He42021,Shi2019,Shi22021,Yang22022}. Gao and He \cite{Gao2017,Gao22018} presented linearized conservative finite element and mixed finite element methods for the PNP equations, and established unconditionally optimal error estimates for all variables in $l^2(H^1)$ and $l^\infty(L^2)$ norms, but they do not derive the error estimates in $l^\infty(H^1)$ norm. For the convergence analysis and error estimates of some other gradient flow model coupled with fluid motion, such as the Cahn-Hilliard-Hele-Shaw equations and the Cahn-Hilliard-Navier-Stokes system, one can refer to \cite{Chen2016,Chen22019,Liu42017,Diegel2017}.

In this paper, we focus on the error estimates of the linearized backward Euler finite element scheme for the NSPNP equations, and use low order conforming finite element pairs. Firstly, under an inductive hypothesis, we derive the existence and uniqueness of solutions, and the unconditional error estimates of all variables in $l^\infty(L^2)$ norms. Then, we obtain the $l^\infty(H^1)$ and $l^2(H^2)$ norm stability of the finite element solutions. Next, based on the stability, we deduce the unconditional error estimates in $l^\infty(H^1)$ norm. Moreover, the global mass conservation and the energy stability are also derived for the proposed numerical scheme. Finally, several numerical examples are implemented to verify the theoretical analysis. Compared with the results in \cite{Sun2016,He22017,He32018,He42021,Shi2019,Shi22021,Yang22022}, we remove the mesh restriction.

Throughout the article, the notations of the standard Sobolev spaces are used. The generic positive constant $C$ with or without subscript is independent of the mesh size $h$ and the time step $\tau$.

% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Preliminary}
\label{sec:preliminary}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

We introduce the following function spaces
\begin{equation}
  X = H^{1}(\Omega),\quad
  X^{0} = \Bigl\{\,\eta\in H^{1}(\Omega) : \int_{\Omega}\eta\,\mathrm{d}x = 0\,\Bigr\},
  \label{eq:X-space}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  V = \bigl(H^{1}_{0}(\Omega)\bigr)^{d},\quad
  Q = L^{2}_{0}(\Omega) = \Bigl\{\,q\in L^{2}(\Omega) : \int_{\Omega}q\,\mathrm{d}x = 0\,\Bigr\}.
  \label{eq:VQ-space}
\end{equation}

The trilinear forms $b_{1}(\cdot\,;\cdot,\cdot)$ on $V\times V\times V$ and
$b_{2}(\cdot\,;\cdot,\cdot)$ on $V\times X\times X$ are defined by
\begin{equation}
  b_{1}(u;\,v,\,w)
  = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl((u\cdot\nabla)v,\,w\bigr)
  - \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl((u\cdot\nabla)w,\,v\bigr),
  \quad \forall\,u,v,w\in V,
  \label{eq:b1}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  b_{2}(u;\,c,\,\eta)
  = \tfrac{1}{2}(u\cdot\nabla c,\,\eta)
  - \tfrac{1}{2}(u\cdot\nabla\eta,\,c),
  \quad \forall\,u\in V,\;c,\eta\in X.
  \label{eq:b2}
\end{equation}

We recall the following Poincar\'{e}--Friedrichs inequalities
\begin{equation}
  \Vert v\Vert_{1} \le C\,\Vert\nabla v\Vert_{0},
  \quad \forall\,v\in V,
  \label{eq:PF1}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \Vert\varphi\Vert_{1}
  \le C\!\left(\Vert\nabla\varphi\Vert_{0}
    + \Bigl|\int_{\Omega}\varphi\,\mathrm{d}x\Bigr|\right),
  \quad \forall\,\varphi\in X,
  \label{eq:PF2}
\end{equation}
and the embedding inequalities
\begin{equation}
  \Vert\varphi\Vert_{0,6} \le C\,\Vert\varphi\Vert_{1},
  \quad \forall\,\varphi\in X.
  \label{eq:embed}
\end{equation}

Next, integrating the third equation of \eqref{eq:NSPNP} and using the
Green formula, we can derive
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}
  \!\left(\int_{\Omega}c_{1}\,\mathrm{d}x\right)
  - \int_{\partial\Omega}\frac{\partial c_{1}}{\partial n}\,\mathrm{d}s
  - \int_{\partial\Omega}c_{1}\frac{\partial\psi}{\partial n}\,\mathrm{d}s
  + \int_{\partial\Omega}u\cdot n\,c_{1}\,\mathrm{d}s = 0.
  \label{eq:c1-int1}
\end{equation}
Then in view of the boundary conditions, we get
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}t}
  \!\left(\int_{\Omega}c_{1}\,\mathrm{d}x\right) = 0,
  \label{eq:c1-conserve}
\end{equation}
which implies
\begin{equation}
  \int_{\Omega}c_{1}\,\mathrm{d}x = \int_{\Omega}c_{10}\,\mathrm{d}x.
  \label{eq:c1-mass}
\end{equation}
Manipulating the fourth and fifth equations of \eqref{eq:NSPNP} in the
same way, we obtain
\begin{equation}
  \int_{\Omega}c_{2}\,\mathrm{d}x = \int_{\Omega}c_{20}\,\mathrm{d}x,
  \qquad
  \int_{\Omega}c_{1}\,\mathrm{d}x = \int_{\Omega}c_{2}\,\mathrm{d}x.
  \label{eq:c12-mass}
\end{equation}
Set
\begin{equation}
  m_{0}
  = \frac{1}{|\Omega|}\int_{\Omega}c_{10}\,\mathrm{d}x
  = \frac{1}{|\Omega|}\int_{\Omega}c_{20}\,\mathrm{d}x.
  \label{eq:m0}
\end{equation}

Take $\tilde{c}_{1}=c_{1}-m_{0}$, $\tilde{c}_{2}=c_{2}-m_{0}$; then we
obtain the variational form of equations \eqref{eq:NSPNP}: find
$(u,p,\tilde{c}_{1},\tilde{c}_{2},\psi)\in V\times Q\times X^{0}\times X^{0}\times X^{0}$
such that
\begin{equation}
  \left\{
  \begin{aligned}
    &(u_{t},v) + (\nabla u,\nabla v) + b_{1}(u;u,v)
      - (\operatorname{div}v,\,p)
      = -\bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}-\tilde{c}_{2})\nabla\psi,\,v\bigr),
      &&\forall\,v\in V,\\[4pt]
    &(\operatorname{div}u,\,q) = 0,
      &&\forall\,q\in Q,\\[4pt]
    &(\tilde{c}_{1t},\,\eta_{1})
      + (\nabla\tilde{c}_{1},\nabla\eta_{1})
      + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}+m_{0})\nabla\psi,\,\nabla\eta_{1}\bigr)
      + b_{2}(u;\tilde{c}_{1},\eta_{1}) = 0,
      &&\forall\,\eta_{1}\in X,\\[4pt]
    &(\tilde{c}_{2t},\,\eta_{2})
      + (\nabla\tilde{c}_{2},\nabla\eta_{2})
      - \bigl((\tilde{c}_{2}+m_{0})\nabla\psi,\,\nabla\eta_{2}\bigr)
      + b_{2}(u;\tilde{c}_{2},\eta_{2}) = 0,
      &&\forall\,\eta_{2}\in X,\\[4pt]
    &(\nabla\psi,\nabla\varphi) = (\tilde{c}_{1}-\tilde{c}_{2},\,\varphi),
      &&\forall\,\varphi\in X.
  \end{aligned}
  \right.
  \label{eq:variational}
\end{equation}

Let $\mathcal{T}_{h}$ be a regular and quasi-uniform triangular or
tetrahedral partition of $\Omega$. We consider low-order conforming finite
element spaces
$V_{h}\times Q_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}
\subset V\times Q\times X^{0}\times X^{0}\times X^{0}$,
which satisfy the following approximation properties: for
$u,\tilde{c}_{1},\tilde{c}_{2},\psi\in H^{2}(\Omega)$,
$p\in H^{1}(\Omega)$, there exist
$v_{h},q_{h},\chi_{1h},\chi_{2h},\varphi_{h}
\in V_{h}\times Q_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}$
such that
\begin{equation}
  \Vert\tilde{c}_{i}-\chi_{ih}\Vert_{0}
  + h\,\Vert\nabla(\tilde{c}_{i}-\chi_{ih})\Vert_{0}
  \le Ch^{2}\Vert\tilde{c}_{i}\Vert_{2},
  \quad i=1,2,
  \label{eq:approx-c}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \Vert\psi-\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0}
  + h\,\Vert\nabla(\psi-\varphi_{h})\Vert_{0}
  \le Ch^{2}\Vert\psi\Vert_{2},
  \label{eq:approx-psi}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \Vert u-v_{h}\Vert_{0}
  + h\bigl(\Vert\nabla(u-v_{h})\Vert_{0}
         + \Vert p-q_{h}\Vert_{0}\bigr)
  \le Ch^{2}\bigl(\Vert u\Vert_{2}+\Vert p\Vert_{1}\bigr),
  \label{eq:approx-u}
\end{equation}
and the inf-sup condition \cite{John2016}
\begin{equation}
  \sup_{v_{h}\in V_{h}}
  \frac{(\operatorname{div}v_{h},\,q_{h})}{\Vert\nabla v_{h}\Vert_{0}}
  \ge C\,\Vert q_{h}\Vert_{0},
  \quad \forall\,q_{h}\in Q_{h}.
  \label{eq:infsup}
\end{equation}

We define the Ritz projection
$R_{h}:X^{0}\to X^{0}_{h}$ and the Stokes projection
$S_{h}:V\times Q\to V_{h}\times Q_{h}$ by
\begin{equation}
  \bigl(\nabla(R_{h}\eta-\eta),\,\nabla\chi_{h}\bigr) = 0,
  \quad \forall\,\chi_{h}\in X^{0}_{h},
  \label{eq:Ritz}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
  \left\{
  \begin{aligned}
    &(\nabla S_{h}u,\nabla v_{h}) - (\operatorname{div}v_{h},S_{h}p)
      = (\nabla u,\nabla v_{h}) - (\operatorname{div}v_{h},p),
      &&\forall\,v_{h}\in V_{h},\\
    &(\operatorname{div}S_{h}u,\,q_{h}) = 0,
      &&\forall\,q_{h}\in Q_{h}.
  \end{aligned}
  \right.
  \label{eq:Stokes-proj}
\end{equation}

Then, from the standard finite element theory \cite{John2016,Li2021}, we have
\begin{equation}
  \Vert\eta - R_{h}\eta\Vert_{0}
  + h\,\Vert\nabla(\eta-R_{h}\eta)\Vert_{0}
  \le Ch^{2}\Vert\eta\Vert_{2},
  \label{eq:Ritz-est}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \Vert u-S_{h}u\Vert_{0}
  + h\bigl(\Vert\nabla(u-S_{h}u)\Vert_{0}
    + \Vert p-S_{h}p\Vert_{0}\bigr)
  \le Ch^{2}\bigl(\Vert u\Vert_{2}+\Vert p\Vert_{1}\bigr),
  \label{eq:Stokes-est}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \Vert u_{t} - S_{h}u_{t}\Vert_{0}
  \le Ch^{2}\bigl(\Vert u_{t}\Vert_{2}+\Vert p_{t}\Vert_{1}\bigr).
  \label{eq:Stokes-t-est}
\end{equation}

The discrete operator $\Delta_{h}:X^{0}_{h}\to X^{0}_{h}$ is given as follows
\begin{equation}
  (\Delta_{h}\eta_{h},\chi_{h})
  = (\nabla\eta_{h},\nabla\chi_{h}),
  \quad \forall\,\chi_{h}\in X^{0}_{h}.
  \label{eq:Deltah}
\end{equation}
The subspace $V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}$ of $V_{h}$ is introduced as
\begin{equation}
  V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}
  = \bigl\{v_{h}\in V_{h} :
    (\operatorname{div}v_{h},q_{h})=0,\;\forall\,q_{h}\in Q_{h}\bigr\}.
  \label{eq:Vhdiv}
\end{equation}
And the discrete Stokes operator $A_{h}:V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}\to V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}$
is defined by
\begin{equation}
  (A_{h}u_{h},v_{h}) = (\nabla u_{h},\nabla v_{h}),
  \quad \forall\,v_{h}\in V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}.
  \label{eq:Ah}
\end{equation}

Then, we have the following Lemmas~\ref{lem:1}--\ref{lem:4}, which can be
found in \cite{Liu2017ch,Diegel2017,Heywood1982,Heywood1990}, or proven
similarly.

\begin{lemma}
  \label{lem:1}
  For $\chi_{h}\in X^{0}_{h}$, there holds
  \begin{equation}
    \Vert\chi_{h}\Vert_{0,\infty}
    + \Vert\nabla\chi_{h}\Vert_{0,6}
    \le C\,\Vert\Delta_{h}\chi_{h}\Vert_{0}.
    \label{eq:lem1}
  \end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{lem:2}
  For $u_{h},v_{h},w_{h}\in V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}$, there hold
  \begin{align}
    b_{1}(u_{h};v_{h},w_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert\nabla u_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla v_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla w_{h}\Vert_{0},
    \label{eq:b1-est1}\\
    b_{1}(u_{h};v_{h},w_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert u_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla v_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert A_{h}v_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert\nabla w_{h}\Vert_{0},
    \label{eq:b1-est2}\\
    b_{1}(u_{h};v_{h},w_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert\nabla u_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla v_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert A_{h}v_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert w_{h}\Vert_{0},
    \label{eq:b1-est3}\\
    b_{1}(u_{h};v_{h},w_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert\nabla u_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert A_{h}u_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert\nabla v_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert w_{h}\Vert_{0},
    \label{eq:b1-est4}\\
    b_{1}(u_{h};v_{h},w_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert u_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla u_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert A_{h}v_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert w_{h}\Vert_{0}.
    \label{eq:b1-est5}
  \end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{lem:3}
  For $u_{h}\in V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}$, $\varphi_{h},\chi_{h}\in X^{0}_{h}$,
  there hold
  \begin{align}
    b_{2}(u_{h};\varphi_{h},\chi_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert\nabla u_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla\chi_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0},
    \label{eq:b2-est1}\\
    b_{2}(u_{h};\varphi_{h},\chi_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert u_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert\Delta_{h}\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert\nabla\chi_{h}\Vert_{0},
    \label{eq:b2-est2}\\
    b_{2}(u_{h};\varphi_{h},\chi_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert\nabla u_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert A_{h}u_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert\nabla\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\chi_{h}\Vert_{0},
    \label{eq:b2-est3}\\
    b_{2}(u_{h};\varphi_{h},\chi_{h})
    &\le C\,\Vert\nabla u_{h}\Vert_{0}
            \Vert\nabla\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert\Delta_{h}\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
            \Vert\chi_{h}\Vert_{0}.
    \label{eq:b2-est4}
  \end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}
  \label{lem:4}
  For $\eta_{h},\varphi_{h},\chi_{h}\in X^{0}_{h}$, there holds
  \begin{equation}
    (\eta_{h}\nabla\varphi_{h},\nabla\chi_{h})
    \le C\,\Vert\nabla\eta_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
           \Vert\Delta_{h}\eta_{h}\Vert_{0}^{1/2}
           \Vert\Delta_{h}\varphi_{h}\Vert_{0}
           \Vert\chi_{h}\Vert_{0}.
    \label{eq:lem4}
  \end{equation}
\end{lemma}

Finally, assume that the solutions and initial values of equations
\eqref{eq:NSPNP} satisfy the following regularity condition:
\begin{equation}
  \begin{aligned}
    &\Vert u_{0}\Vert_{2}^{2}
     + \Vert c_{10}\Vert_{2}^{2}
     + \Vert c_{20}\Vert_{2}^{2}
     + \Vert c_{1}\Vert_{L^{\infty}(H^{2})}^{2}
     + \Vert c_{2}\Vert_{L^{\infty}(H^{2})}^{2}
     + \Vert\psi\Vert_{L^{\infty}(H^{3})}^{2}\\
    &\quad
     + \Vert u\Vert_{L^{\infty}(H^{2})}^{2}
     + \Vert p\Vert_{L^{\infty}(H^{1})}^{2}
     + \Vert c_{1t}\Vert_{L^{2}(H^{2})}^{2}
     + \Vert c_{1tt}\Vert_{L^{2}(L^{2})}^{2}

     + \Vert c_{2tt}\Vert_{L^{2}(L^{2})}^{2}

     + \Vert u_{tt}\Vert_{L^{2}(L^{2})}^{2}
     + \Vert p_{t}\Vert_{L^{2}(H^{1})}^{2}
    \le C_{0}.
  \end{aligned}
  \label{eq:regularity}
\end{equation}

% ------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{The linearized backward Euler scheme}
\label{sec:scheme}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------

Let $\{t_{j}: t_{j}=j\tau,\;0\le j\le J\}$ be a uniform partition of the
time interval $[0,T]$ with time step $\tau=T/J$. Denote
$f^{j}=f(\cdot,t_{j})$, and for a sequence of functions $\{f^{j}\}_{j=0}^{J}$
we define
\begin{equation}
  D_{\tau}f^{j+1} = \frac{f^{j+1}-f^{j}}{\tau}.
  \label{eq:Dtau}
\end{equation}

With the above notation, the linearized backward Euler scheme of
\eqref{eq:variational} is given as follows.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~1.}
Set $(\tilde{c}^{0}_{1h},\tilde{c}^{0}_{2h},u^{0}_{h})
=(R_{h}\tilde{c}_{10},R_{h}\tilde{c}_{20},S_{h}u_{0})$.
For $j=0,1,\ldots,J-1$, find
$(u^{j+1}_{h},p^{j+1}_{h},\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h},
 \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h},\psi^{j+1}_{h})
\in V_{h}\times Q_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}$
such that
\begin{equation}
  \left\{
  \begin{aligned}
    &\bigl(D_{\tau}u^{j+1}_{h},v_{h}\bigr)
     + \bigl(\nabla u^{j+1}_{h},\nabla v_{h}\bigr)
     + b_{1}\bigl(u^{j}_{h};\,u^{j+1}_{h},\,v_{h}\bigr)
     - \bigl(\operatorname{div}v_{h},\,p^{j+1}_{h}\bigr)\\
    &\quad
     = -\bigl((\tilde{c}^{j}_{1h}-\tilde{c}^{j}_{2h})\nabla\psi^{j+1}_{h},\,v_{h}\bigr),
     \quad \forall\,v_{h}\in V_{h},\\[4pt]
    &\bigl(\operatorname{div}u^{j+1}_{h},\,q_{h}\bigr) = 0,
     \quad \forall\,q_{h}\in Q_{h},\\[4pt]
    &\bigl(D_{\tau}\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h},\,\eta_{1h}\bigr)
     + \bigl(\nabla\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h},\nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)
     + \bigl((\tilde{c}^{j}_{1h}+m_{0})\nabla\psi^{j+1}_{h},\nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)\\
    &\quad
     + b_{2}\bigl(u^{j}_{h};\,\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h},\,\eta_{1h}\bigr)
     = 0,
     \quad \forall\,\eta_{1h}\in X_{h},\\[4pt]
    &\bigl(D_{\tau}\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h},\,\eta_{2h}\bigr)
     + \bigl(\nabla\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h},\nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)
     - \bigl((\tilde{c}^{j}_{2h}+m_{0})\nabla\psi^{j+1}_{h},\nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)\\
    &\quad
     + b_{2}\bigl(u^{j}_{h};\,\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h},\,\eta_{2h}\bigr)
     = 0,
     \quad \forall\,\eta_{2h}\in X_{h},\\[4pt]
    &\bigl(\nabla\psi^{j+1}_{h},\nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
     = \bigl(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h},\,\varphi_{h}\bigr),
     \quad \forall\,\varphi_{h}\in X_{h}.
  \end{aligned}
  \right.
  \label{eq:scheme}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{Step~2.} Set
\begin{equation}
  c^{j+1}_{1h} = \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h} + m_{0},
  \qquad
  c^{j+1}_{2h} = \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h} + m_{0}.
  \label{eq:c-recover}
\end{equation}

To derive the well-posedness and unconditional error estimates of scheme
\eqref{eq:scheme}, we need the following inductive hypothesis:
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j}_{1h}\bigr\Vert_{0,3}
  + \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j}_{2h}\bigr\Vert_{0,3}
  \le C_{1}.
  \label{eq:inductive}
\end{equation}

In the remainder of this section we prove the existence and uniqueness of
scheme \eqref{eq:scheme}.

\begin{theorem}
  \label{thm:wellposed}
  If the time step $\tau$ is small enough, scheme \eqref{eq:scheme} has a
  unique solution.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since scheme \eqref{eq:scheme} is an algebraic linear system, we only need
to prove uniqueness. Suppose there exist
$(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,i},\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,i},\psi^{j+1}_{h,i})
\in X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}\times X^{0}_{h}$, $i=1,2$, satisfying the
last three equations of \eqref{eq:scheme}. Then we have
\begin{equation}
  \left\{
  \begin{aligned}
    &\bigl(D_{\tau}(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}),\,\eta_{1h}\bigr)
     + \bigl(\nabla(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}),\nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)\\
    &\quad
     + \bigl((\tilde{c}^{j}_{1h}+m_{0})
       \nabla(\psi^{j+1}_{h,1}-\psi^{j+1}_{h,2}),\nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)
     + b_{2}\bigl(u^{j}_{h};\,
       \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2},\,\eta_{1h}\bigr) = 0,\\[4pt]
    &\bigl(D_{\tau}(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}),\,\eta_{2h}\bigr)
     + \bigl(\nabla(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}),\nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)\\
    &\quad
     - \bigl((\tilde{c}^{j}_{2h}+m_{0})
       \nabla(\psi^{j+1}_{h,1}-\psi^{j+1}_{h,2}),\nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)
     + b_{2}\bigl(u^{j}_{h};\,
       \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2},\,\eta_{2h}\bigr) = 0,\\[4pt]
    &\bigl(\nabla(\psi^{j+1}_{h,1}-\psi^{j+1}_{h,2}),\nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
     = \bigl((\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2})
       - (\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}),\,\varphi_{h}\bigr).
  \end{aligned}
  \right.
  \label{eq:diff-system}
\end{equation}

Taking $\varphi_{h}=\psi^{j+1}_{h,1}-\psi^{j+1}_{h,2}$ and
$\varphi_{h}=\Delta_{h}(\psi^{j+1}_{h,1}-\psi^{j+1}_{h,2})$ in the third
equation of \eqref{eq:diff-system}, respectively, we obtain
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\Vert\nabla(\psi^{j+1}_{h,1}-\psi^{j+1}_{h,2})\bigr\Vert_{0}
  \le C\bigl(\bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}
           + \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}\bigr),
  \label{eq:psi-diff1}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\Vert\Delta_{h}(\psi^{j+1}_{h,1}-\psi^{j+1}_{h,2})\bigr\Vert_{0}
  \le \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}
    + \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}.
  \label{eq:psi-diff2}
\end{equation}

Setting $\eta_{1h}=\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}$ in the
first equation of \eqref{eq:diff-system} and using
\eqref{eq:lem1}, \eqref{eq:inductive}, \eqref{eq:psi-diff2}, we arrive at
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
  + \tfrac{\tau}{2}\bigl\Vert\nabla(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2})\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
  \le CC_{1}^{2}\tau\bigl(\bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
      + \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}\bigr).
  \label{eq:c1-unique}
\end{equation}

Similarly, we get
\begin{equation}
  \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
  + \tfrac{\tau}{2}\bigl\Vert\nabla(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2})\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
  \le CC_{1}^{2}\tau\bigl(\bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
      + \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}\bigr).
  \label{eq:c2-unique}
\end{equation}

Summing \eqref{eq:c1-unique} and \eqref{eq:c2-unique} gives
\begin{equation}
  \begin{aligned}
    &(1-CC_{1}^{2}\tau)
     \bigl(\bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
           + \bigl\Vert\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2}\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}\bigr)\\
    &\quad
     + \tfrac{\tau}{2}\bigl(\bigl\Vert\nabla(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2})\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}
           + \bigl\Vert\nabla(\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1}-\tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2})\bigr\Vert_{0}^{2}\bigr)
     \le 0.
  \end{aligned}
  \label{eq:c12-sum}
\end{equation}

Setting $\tau<\tau_{1}:=\dfrac{1}{2CC_{1}^{2}}$ in \eqref{eq:c12-sum} yields
\begin{equation}
  \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,1} = \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{1h,2},
  \qquad
  \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,1} = \tilde{c}^{j+1}_{2h,2},
  \label{eq:c-unique}
\end{equation}
which together with \eqref{eq:psi-diff1} leads to
\begin{equation}
  \psi^{j+1}_{h,1} = \psi^{j+1}_{h,2}.
  \label{eq:psi-unique}
\end{equation}
Finally, the first two equations of \eqref{eq:scheme} represent the
linearized backward Euler scheme of the Navier--Stokes equations, which has
the unique solution $(u^{j+1}_{h},p^{j+1}_{h})$. The proof is completed.
\end{proof}

\section{The error estimates}\label{sec:error}

In this section, we will derive the unconditional error estimates of all the variables. Let
\begin{equation}\label{eq:error-def}
\begin{aligned}
  e_{u}^{j+1}       &= u_{h}^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1}, \quad
  e_{p}^{j+1}        = p_{h}^{j+1} - S_{h}p^{j+1}, \\
  e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1} &= \tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}, \quad
  e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}  = \tilde{c}_{2h}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1}, \quad
  e_{\psi}^{j+1}     = \psi_{h}^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1},
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
and subtract \eqref{eq:scheme} from \eqref{eq:variational} with $t = t_{j+1}$, then we obtain the error equations
\begin{align}
  &\bigl(u_{t}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}u^{j+1},\, v_{h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(D_{\tau}(u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1}),\, v_{h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1},\, v_{h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\nabla e_{u}^{j+1},\, \nabla v_{h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{1}(u^{j+1}-u^{j};\, u^{j+1},\, v_{h})
   + b_{1}(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j};\, u^{j+1},\, v_{h})
   + b_{1}(S_{h}u^{j};\, u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1},\, v_{h}) \nonumber\\
  &\quad - b_{1}(e_{u}^{j};\, S_{h}u^{j+1},\, v_{h})
   - b_{1}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{u}^{j+1},\, v_{h})
   + \bigl(e_{p}^{j+1},\, \operatorname{div} v_{h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla\psi^{j+1}
             - \tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, v_{h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1}\nabla\psi^{j+1}
             - \tilde{c}_{2h}^{j}\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, v_{h}\bigr)
   = 0, \quad \forall\, v_{h}\in V_{h},
  \label{eq:err-u}\\[4pt]
  &\bigl(\operatorname{div} e_{u}^{j+1},\, q_{h}\bigr) = 0,
   \quad \forall\, q_{h}\in Q_{h},
  \label{eq:err-divu}\\[4pt]
  &\bigl(\tilde{c}_{1t}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad - m_{0}\bigl(\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)
   + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)
   + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad - \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\, \nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{1h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{2}(u^{j+1}-u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h})
   + b_{2}(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h})
   - b_{2}(e_{u}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h}) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{2}(u_{h}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h})
   - b_{2}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1},\, \eta_{1h})
   = 0, \quad \forall\, \eta_{1h}\in X_{h},
  \label{eq:err-c1}\\[4pt]
  &\bigl(\tilde{c}_{2t}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + m_{0}\bigl(\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)
   - \bigl((\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{2}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)
   - \bigl((\tilde{c}_{2}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j}\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\, \nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j}\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, \nabla\eta_{2h}\bigr) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{2}(u^{j+1}-u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h})
   + b_{2}(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h})
   - b_{2}(e_{u}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h}) \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{2}(u_{h}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h})
   - b_{2}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1},\, \eta_{2h})
   = 0, \quad \forall\, \eta_{2h}\in X_{h},
  \label{eq:err-c2}\\[4pt]
  &\bigl(\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
   = -\bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, \varphi_{h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1},\, \varphi_{h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1} - e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1},\, \varphi_{h}\bigr),
   \quad \forall\, \varphi_{h}\in X_{h}.
  \label{eq:err-psi}
\end{align}

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$\ell^{\infty}(L^{2})$}{l-inf(L2)}-norm error estimates}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

Now, we derive the $\ell^{\infty}(L^{2})$-norm error estimates of all variables, which are shown in
following Lemmas~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}--\ref{lem:eu-l2} and Theorem~\ref{thm:l2}.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:epsi-l2}
Assume that $(\tilde{c}_{1},\tilde{c}_{2},\psi,u,p)$ and
$(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1},\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j+1},\psi_{h}^{j+1},u_{h}^{j+1},p_{h}^{j+1})$
are solutions of \eqref{eq:variational} and \eqref{eq:scheme}, respectively, and the regularity
condition \eqref{eq:regularity} holds. Then we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:epsi-l2}
  \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|\Delta_{h} e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{0}h^{2}
  + C\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
           + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr).
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Taking $\varphi_{h} = e_{\psi}^{j+1}$ and $\varphi_{h} = \Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{j+1}$
in \eqref{eq:err-psi}, respectively, we get
\begin{align}
  \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &\leq C\Bigl(\bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
              + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0} \nonumber\\
  &\quad + C\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
                 + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
            \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0},
  \label{eq:grad-epsi}\\[4pt]
  \bigl\|\Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &\leq \Bigl(\bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
             + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
        \bigl\|\Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0} \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
                + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
           \bigl\|\Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0},
  \label{eq:Deltah-epsi}
\end{align}
which implies the desired result \eqref{eq:epsi-l2}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:ec-l2}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we have
\begin{align}
  &\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                               + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\qquad \leq CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
   + C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})\,\tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}
     \Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
          + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
          + \bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr).
  \label{eq:ec-l2}
\end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Setting $\eta_{1h} = e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}$ in \eqref{eq:err-c1} leads to
\begin{align}
  &\frac{1}{2\tau}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                       + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1} - e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                       - \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
   + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &= \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1t}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + \bigl(D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - m_{0}\bigl(\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}-\tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\, \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{2}(u^{j+1}-u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
   + b_{2}(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
   - b_{2}(e_{u}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{2}(u_{h}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
   \;:=\; \sum_{i=1}^{12} E_{i}.
  \label{eq:E-sum}
\end{align}
Now, we bound the above terms. From the Taylor formula, Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2} and \eqref{eq:Ritz-error}, it is not hard to check that
\begin{align}
  \sum_{i=1}^{3}|E_{i}|
  &\leq C\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1tt}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
   + \frac{Ch^{4}}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{2}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
   + CC_{0}^{2}h^{4} \nonumber\\
  &\quad + C\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                 + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
   + \frac{1}{7}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:E123}
\end{align}
and
\begin{align}
  \sum_{i=4}^{6}|E_{i}|
  &\leq \Bigl(\bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}-\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
             + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
             + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\nabla\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
    \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\!\left(\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
    + C_{0}^{2}h^{4}
    + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\right)
   + \frac{1}{7}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:E456}
\end{align}
By use of the inductive hypothesis \eqref{eq:inductive}, Lemma~\ref{lem:Deltah-bound} and
Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we obtain
\begin{align}
  |E_{7}|
  &\leq \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,3}
         \bigl\|\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0,6}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{0}^{2}C_{1}^{2}h^{2}
       + \frac{1}{7}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:E7}\\[4pt]
  |E_{8}|
  &\leq \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,3}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,6}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{0}^{2}C_{1}^{2}h^{4}
       + CC_{1}^{2}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                         + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
       + \frac{1}{7}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:E8}
\end{align}
According to Lemma~\ref{lem:b2-bounds}, we infer
\begin{align}
  \sum_{i=9}^{11}|E_{i}|
  &\leq C\Bigl(\bigl\|u^{j+1}-u^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
              + \bigl\|u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
              + \bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{2}
    \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\!\left(\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|u_{t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
    + C_{0}^{2}h^{4}
    + \bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\right)
   + \frac{1}{7}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:E9-11}
\end{align}
and
\begin{align}
  |E_{12}|
  &\leq \bigl|b_{2}(e_{u}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\,
                                   e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})\bigr|
       + \bigl|b_{2}(S_{h}u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\,
                                    e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})\bigr|
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
       + \|A_{h}S_{h}u^{j}\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
       + CC_{0}^{4}h^{4}
       + \frac{1}{7}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:E12}
\end{align}
Inserting \eqref{eq:E123}--\eqref{eq:E12} into \eqref{eq:E-sum}, and summing up from $j = 0$
to $j = J-1$, we get
\begin{align}
  \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  + \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})\,\tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}
    \Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
         + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
         + \bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr).
  \label{eq:ec1-l2-sum}
\end{align}
Subsequently, the similar estimates for $e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}$ can also be obtained,
which together with \eqref{eq:ec1-l2-sum} lead to \eqref{eq:ec-l2}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:eu-l2}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we have
\begin{align}
  \bigl\|e_{u}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  + \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})(\tau^{2}+h^{4})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})\,\tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}
    \Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
         + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
         + \bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr).
  \label{eq:eu-l2}
\end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Choose $v_{h} = e_{u}^{j+1} \in V_{h}^{\mathrm{div}}$ in \eqref{eq:err-u}, and note that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:b1-cancel}
  b_{1}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{u}^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}) = 0, \qquad
  \bigl(e_{p}^{j+1},\, \operatorname{div} e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr) = 0,
\end{equation}
then we arrive at
\begin{align}
  &\frac{1}{2\tau}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                       + \bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1} - e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                       - \bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
   + \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &= \bigl(u_{t}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}u^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + \bigl(D_{\tau}(u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1}),\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + b_{1}(u^{j+1}-u^{j};\, u^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{1}(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j};\, u^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1})
   + b_{1}(S_{h}u^{j};\, u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1})
   - b_{1}(e_{u}^{j};\, S_{h}u^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla\psi^{j+1}
              - \tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1}\nabla\psi^{j+1}
              - \tilde{c}_{2h}^{j}\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   \;:=\; \sum_{i=1}^{8}G_{i}.
  \label{eq:G-sum}
\end{align}
By use of Taylor formula and Poincar\'{e} inequality, it is easy to check that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:G1}
  |G_{1}| \leq C\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|u_{tt}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
             + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
Note that
\begin{align}
  \bigl\|D_{\tau}(u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &= \left\|\frac{(u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1}) - (u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j})}{\tau}\right\|_{0}^{2}
   = \left\|\frac{1}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}(u - S_{h}u)_{t}\,\mathrm{d}t\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq \left\|\frac{1}{\tau}\left(\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}1^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t\right)^{\!1/2}
         \left(\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|(u - S_{h}u)_{t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t\right)^{\!1/2}
        \right\|_{0}^{2}
  \leq \frac{1}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|(u - S_{h}u)_{t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t,
  \label{eq:Dtau-Shu}
\end{align}
then using \eqref{eq:Stokes-t-error}, we find
\begin{equation}\label{eq:G2}
  |G_{2}| \leq \frac{Ch^{4}}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}
               \bigl(\|u_{t}\|_{2} + \|p_{t}\|_{1}\bigr)\,\mathrm{d}t
             + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
Due to Lemma~\ref{lem:b1-bounds} and Taylor formula, we deduce
\begin{equation}\label{eq:G3G4}
  |G_{3}| + |G_{4}|
  \leq C\Bigl(\bigl\|u^{j+1}-u^{j}\bigr\|_{0} + \bigl\|u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
        \bigl\|u^{j+1}\bigr\|_{2}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|u_{t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
     + CC_{0}^{4}h^{4}
     + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
\end{equation}
and
\begin{align}
  |G_{5}| &\leq C\|A_{h}S_{h}u^{j}\|_{0}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
                  \bigl\|u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
           \leq CC_{0}^{4}h^{4} + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:G5}\\[4pt]
  |G_{6}| &\leq C\bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}\|A_{h}S_{h}u^{j+1}\|_{0}
                  \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
           \leq CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
              + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:G6}
\end{align}
Next, $G_{7}$ can be split as
\begin{align}
  G_{7} &= \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
          + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
          + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad - \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
          - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\, e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
         \;:=\; \sum_{i=1}^{5}G_{7i}.
  \label{eq:G7-split}
\end{align}
It is not hard to find that
\begin{align}
  |G_{71}| + |G_{73}| + |G_{74}| + |G_{75}|
  &\leq \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,6}
       + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,6}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
           \bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,6}
         + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,3}
           \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,6}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
    + CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})h^{4}
    + CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + CC_{1}^{2}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                           + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
         + \frac{1}{16}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:G71-75}
\end{align}
Using Green formula, we obtain
\begin{align}
  |G_{72}|
  &= \bigl|\bigl(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\,
                 \nabla\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\cdot e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
          + \bigl(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\,
                 \tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\operatorname{div}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)\bigr|
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq \bigl\|\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
    \Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,3}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,6}
         + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}\bigl\|\operatorname{div}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
  \leq CC_{0}^{4}h^{4} + \frac{1}{16}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:G72}
\end{align}
Combining \eqref{eq:G71-75} and \eqref{eq:G72}, we have
\begin{align}
  |G_{7}|
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
    + CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})h^{4}
    + CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + CC_{1}^{2}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                           + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
         + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:G7}
\end{align}
In a similar way, $G_{8}$ can be estimated as
\begin{align}
  |G_{8}|
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{2t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
    + CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})h^{4}
    + CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + CC_{1}^{2}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                           + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
         + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:G8}
\end{align}
Therefore, inserting estimates \eqref{eq:G1}, \eqref{eq:G2}--\eqref{eq:G6}, \eqref{eq:G7},
\eqref{eq:G8} into \eqref{eq:G-sum}, we get
\begin{align}
  &\frac{1}{2\tau}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                       - \bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
   + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})h^{4}
   + \frac{Ch^{4}}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}
     \bigl(\|u_{t}\|_{2}^{2} + \|p_{t}\|_{1}^{2}\bigr)\,\mathrm{d}t
   + CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}
     \bigl(\|u_{tt}\|_{0}^{2} + \|u_{t}\|_{0}^{2}
          + \|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{0}^{2} + \|\tilde{c}_{2t}\|_{0}^{2}\bigr)\,\mathrm{d}t
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                                  + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                                  + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                                  + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr).
  \label{eq:eu-step}
\end{align}
Finally, summing \eqref{eq:eu-step} up from $j = 0$ to $J-1$, we arrive at \eqref{eq:eu-l2}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:l2}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, if $\tau$ is small enough, we have
\begin{align}
  &\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \bigl\|e_{u}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                               + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                               + \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
   \leq C_{2}^{2}\bigl(\tau^{2} + h^{2}\bigr),
  \label{eq:l2-result}\\[4pt]
  &\bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|\Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}
   \leq C_{2}(\tau + h),
  \label{eq:l2-psi}
\end{align}
where $C_{2}^{2} = CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})\exp\!\bigl(CC_{0}^{2}+CC_{1}^{2}\bigr)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Using Lemma~\ref{lem:ec-l2} and Lemma~\ref{lem:eu-l2}, we get
\begin{align}
  &\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \bigl\|e_{u}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                               + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                               + \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\qquad \leq CC_{0}^{2}(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
   + C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})\,\tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}
     \Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
          + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
          + \bigl\|e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr).
  \label{eq:combined-l2}
\end{align}
By the Gronwall lemma, taking $\tau < \tau_{2} := \dfrac{1}{C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})}$,
we deduce \eqref{eq:l2-result}. Then using Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we obtain \eqref{eq:l2-psi}.
\end{proof}

The error estimates in Lemmas~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}--\ref{lem:eu-l2} and Theorem~\ref{thm:l2}
were derived under the inductive hypothesis \eqref{eq:inductive}. Now, we check it.

When $j = 0$, we deduce
\begin{equation}\label{eq:j0-check}
  \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{0}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{2h}^{0}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  = \|R_{h}\tilde{c}_{10}\|_{0,3} + \|R_{h}\tilde{c}_{20}\|_{0,3}
  < 1 + C_{0} =: C_{1}.
\end{equation}
Assume that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:inductive-m}
  \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{m}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{2h}^{m}\bigr\|_{0,3} \leq C_{1}
\end{equation}
is true for $m \leq j$. Next, we derive \eqref{eq:inductive} for $m = j+1$. To this aim, we
consider two cases. If $\tau < h$, we have from the inverse inequality and Theorem~\ref{thm:l2} that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:case1}
  \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  \leq Ch^{-d/6}\Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
                     + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
  \leq CC_{2}h^{1/2}.
\end{equation}
If $h < \tau$, by Theorem~\ref{thm:l2}, we deduce
\begin{equation}\label{eq:case2}
  \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  \leq C\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
             + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
  \leq CC_{2}\tau^{1/2}.
\end{equation}
In any case, if $\tau < \tau_{3} := \dfrac{1}{C^{2}C_{2}^{2}}$ and
$h < h_{1} := \dfrac{1}{C^{2}C_{2}^{2}}$, we conclude that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:err-small}
  \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3} < 1.
\end{equation}
Thus, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:inductive-verified}
  \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  \leq \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
     + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
     + \bigl\|R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
     + \bigl\|R_{h}\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,3}
  \leq 1 + C_{0} = C_{1}.
\end{equation}
The check of the inductive hypothesis \eqref{eq:inductive} is completed.

\begin{remark}\label{rem:inductive}
In the using of the mathematical inductive method, it is crucial to bound
$\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\|_{0,3}$ and $\|\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j+1}\|_{0,3}$ to an appointed
constant, otherwise the error bounds in Lemmas~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}--\ref{lem:eu-l2} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:l2} may become larger and larger.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:suboptimal}
Theorem~\ref{thm:l2} shows that the error estimates of the $\ell^{\infty}(L^{2})$ norm are
suboptimal, which is caused by the fact that the estimate of the term $E_{7}$ in
Lemma~\ref{lem:ec-l2} cannot achieve optimal.
\end{remark}

Finally, we derive the unconditional stability of solutions.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:stability}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, if $\tau$ and $h$ are small enough, we have
\begin{align}
  \bigl\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  &\leq C_{3},
  \label{eq:stab-psi}\\[4pt]
  \bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|\nabla u_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  &\leq C_{3},
  \label{eq:stab-H1}\\[4pt]
  \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\Bigl(\bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                            + \bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                            + \bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
  &\leq C_{4}^{2},
  \label{eq:stab-H2}
\end{align}
where $C_{3} = CC_{2} + 2C_{0}$, $C_{4}^{2} = CC_{3}^{6} + CC_{0}^{2}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
First of all, from the definition of the Ritz projection \eqref{eq:Ritz-def}, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Ritz-prop}
  \bigl(\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\chi_{h}\bigr)
  = \bigl(\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\chi_{h}\bigr), \quad \forall\,\chi_{h}\in X_{h}^{0}.
\end{equation}
Taking $\chi_{h} = R_{h}\psi^{j+1}$ and $\chi_{h} = \Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}$ in
\eqref{eq:Ritz-prop}, respectively, and using \eqref{eq:Deltah-def}, we infer
\begin{align}
  \bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &= \bigl(\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr)
   \leq \bigl\|\nabla\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0},
  \label{eq:Ritz-grad}\\[4pt]
  \bigl\|\Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &= \bigl(\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr)
   = \bigl(\nabla\psi^{j+1},\, \nabla\Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &= -\bigl(\Delta\psi^{j+1},\, \Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr)
   \leq \bigl\|\Delta\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}\bigl\|\Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0},
  \label{eq:Ritz-Deltah}
\end{align}
which implies
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Ritz-bound}
  \bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0} \leq \bigl\|\nabla\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}, \qquad
  \bigl\|\Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0} \leq \bigl\|\Delta\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}.
\end{equation}
Then using \eqref{eq:l2-psi} in Theorem~\ref{thm:l2}, we deduce
\begin{align}
  \bigl\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  &= \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|\Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|\Delta_{h}R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq C_{2}(\tau+h) + 2\bigl\|\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{2}
   \leq CC_{2} + 2C_{0} =: C_{3}.
  \label{eq:stab-psi-proof}
\end{align}
Next, if $\tau < h$, from the inverse inequality and the $\ell^{\infty}(L^{2})$ norm estimate
in \eqref{eq:l2-result}, we infer
\begin{equation}\label{eq:grad-ec1-case1}
  \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq Ch^{-1}\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{2}h^{-1}(\tau+h) \leq CC_{2}.
\end{equation}
If $h < \tau$, by use of the $\ell^{2}(H^{1})$ norm estimate in \eqref{eq:l2-result}, we deduce
\begin{equation}\label{eq:grad-ec1-case2}
  \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq C_{2}\tau^{-1/2}(\tau+h) \leq CC_{2}\tau^{1/2}.
\end{equation}
In any case, we obtain
\begin{equation}\label{eq:stab-c1}
  \bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
     + \bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{2} + C_{0} =: C_{3}.
\end{equation}
Similarly, we can derive the bounds of $\ell^{\infty}(H^{1})$ norm for
$\tilde{c}_{2h}^{j+1}$ and $u_{h}^{j+1}$, which implies \eqref{eq:stab-H1}.

Finally, taking $\eta_{1h} = 2\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\tau$ in the third equation
of \eqref{eq:scheme}, and using \eqref{eq:Deltah-def}, \eqref{eq:b1-b2-estimate3},
\eqref{eq:Lemma4-bound}, we have
\begin{align}
  &\bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   - \bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \bigl\|\nabla(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1h}^{j})\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + 2\tau\bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq 2\tau\bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\,
                   \nabla\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr)
       + 2\tau m_{0}\bigl(\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\,
                           \nabla\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr)
       + 2\tau b_{2}\bigl(u_{h}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1},\,
                           \Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq C\tau\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{1/2}
                   \bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{1/2}
                  + m_{0}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
    \bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + C\tau\bigl\|\nabla u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
    \bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{1/2}
    \bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{3/2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{3}^{6}\tau + \tau\bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:Deltah-c1h}
\end{align}
Summing \eqref{eq:Deltah-c1h} up from $j = 0$ to $J-1$, we get
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sum-Deltah-c1h}
  \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \leq CC_{3}^{6} + C\bigl\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1h}^{0}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \leq CC_{3}^{6} + CC_{0}^{2} =: C_{4}^{2}.
\end{equation}
In an analogous procedure, we can derive the rest part of \eqref{eq:stab-H2}. The proof is completed.
\end{proof}

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$\ell^{\infty}(H^{1})$}{l-inf(H1)}-norm error estimate}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

In this section, we derive the $\ell^{\infty}(H^{1})$-norm error estimates of all the variables,
which are shown in following Theorems~\ref{thm:H1-u}--\ref{thm:H1-all}.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:H1-u}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H1-u}
  \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  + \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \leq C_{5}^{2}\bigl(\tau^{2} + h^{2}\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $C_{5}^{2} = C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})C_{2}^{2}\exp\!\bigl(CC_{0}^{2}+CC_{4}^{2}\bigr)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Putting $v_{h} = D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}$ in \eqref{eq:err-u} results in
\begin{align}
  &\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \frac{1}{2\tau}\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                         - \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq \bigl(u_{t}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}u^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + \bigl(D_{\tau}(u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1}),\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + b_{1}(u^{j+1}-u^{j};\, u^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{1}(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j};\, u^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1})
   + b_{1}(S_{h}u^{j};\, u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1})
   - b_{1}(e_{u}^{j};\, S_{h}u^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad - b_{1}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{u}^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1})
   + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla\psi^{j+1}
             - \tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{2}^{j+1}\nabla\psi^{j+1}
             - \tilde{c}_{2h}^{j}\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
   \;:=\; \sum_{i=1}^{9}L_{i}.
  \label{eq:L-sum}
\end{align}
Now, we estimate the above nine terms. It is not difficult to see that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L1L2}
  |L_{1}| + |L_{2}|
  \leq C\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|u_{tt}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
     + \frac{Ch^{4}}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}
       \bigl(\|u_{t}\|_{2}^{2} + \|p_{t}\|_{1}^{2}\bigr)\,\mathrm{d}t
     + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
In view of Lemma~\ref{lem:b1-bounds}, we infer
\begin{align}
  |L_{3}| + |L_{4}|
  &\leq C\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla(u^{j+1}-u^{j})\bigr\|_{0}
              + \bigl\|\nabla(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j})\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|u^{j+1}\bigr\|_{2}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\nabla u_{t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
     + CC_{0}^{4}h^{2}
     + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:L3L4}
\end{align}
and
\begin{align}
  |L_{5}| &\leq \|A_{h}S_{h}u^{j}\|_{0}
                \bigl\|\nabla(u^{j+1} - S_{h}u^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
                \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
           \leq CC_{0}^{4}h^{2}
              + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:L5}\\[4pt]
  |L_{6}| &\leq \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
                \bigl\|A_{h}S_{h}u^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
                \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
           \leq CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
              + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:L6}\\[4pt]
  |L_{7}| &= \bigl|b_{1}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{u}^{j+1} - e_{u}^{j},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1})
              + b_{1}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{u}^{j},\, D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1})\bigr|
  \nonumber\\
          &\leq 0
             + C\bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
               \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
               \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
          &\leq C\bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
               \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
             + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:L7}
\end{align}
The term $L_{8}$ can be split as
\begin{align}
  L_{8} &= \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\,
                 D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
          + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
                 D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\,
                 D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
          - \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1},\,
                 D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr)
          - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\,
                 D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr).
  \label{eq:L8-split}
\end{align}
Then we deduce
\begin{align}
  L_{8}
  &\leq \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
         \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
       + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
         \bigl\|\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \Bigl(\bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
               + \bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\nabla R_{h}\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
    \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,3}
    \bigl\|\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,6}
    \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
    + C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})C_{2}^{2}(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
    + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:L8}
\end{align}
Similarly, we get
\begin{equation}\label{eq:L9}
  L_{9} \leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{2t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
           + C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})C_{2}^{2}(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
           + \frac{1}{8}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
Inserting estimates \eqref{eq:L1L2}--\eqref{eq:L9} into \eqref{eq:L-sum}, and then summing up
from $j = 0$ to $J-1$, we have
\begin{align}
  \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{u}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  + \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  &\leq C(C_{0}^{2}+C_{1}^{2})C_{2}^{2}(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
   + C\tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\Bigl(C_{0}^{2}
                                 + \bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:H1-u-pre}
\end{align}
Finally, by the Gronwall lemma and \eqref{eq:stab-H2}, we conclude \eqref{eq:H1-u}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:H1-c}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H1-c}
  \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  + \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\Bigl(\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                              + \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
  \leq C_{6}^{2}\bigl(\tau^{2} + h^{2}\bigr),
\end{equation}
where $C_{6}^{2} = C(C_{2}^{2}C_{4}^{2} + C_{0}^{2}C_{5}^{2})
\exp\!\bigl(CC_{0}^{2} + CC_{4}^{2}\bigr)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Set $\eta_{1h} = D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}$ in \eqref{eq:err-c1}, and we obtain
\begin{align}
  &\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \frac{1}{2\tau}\Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
                         - \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &= \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1t}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\,
           D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + \bigl(D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - D_{\tau}R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\,
           D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - m_{0}\bigl(\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\,
                 \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\,
               \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\nabla\psi^{j+1},\,
           \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\nabla\psi^{j+1},\,
           \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
               \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   - \bigl(\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\nabla e_{\psi}^{j+1},\,
           \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
   + b_{2}(u^{j+1}-u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + b_{2}(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
   - b_{2}(e_{u}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
   + b_{2}(u_{h}^{j};\, \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1},\,
           D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad - b_{2}(u_{h}^{j};\, e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1},\, D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1})
  \;:=\; \sum_{i=1}^{13}M_{i}.
  \label{eq:M-sum}
\end{align}
It is not hard to find that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:M1M2}
  |M_{1}| + |M_{2}|
  \leq C\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1tt}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
     + \frac{Ch^{4}}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{2}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
     + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:M5}
  |M_{5}|
  \leq C\bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
         \bigl\|\nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{0}^{4}h^{2} + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
According to \eqref{eq:Deltah-def}, we find
\begin{equation}\label{eq:M3}
  |M_{3}|
  \leq m_{0}\bigl\|\Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
             \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{2}^{2}(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
     + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
By use of Green formula, we get
\begin{align}
  |M_{4}|
  &= \Bigl|\bigl(\nabla(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\cdot\nabla\psi^{j+1},\,
                 D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
          + \bigl((\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\Delta\psi^{j+1},\,
                 D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)\Bigr|
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
     + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:M4}
\end{align}
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:M6}
  |M_{6}|
  = \Bigl|\bigl(\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\cdot\nabla\psi^{j+1},\,
               D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)
         + \bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\Delta\psi^{j+1},\,
               D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr)\Bigr|
  \leq CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
     + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
Thanks to Lemma~\ref{lem:Lemma4}, we infer
\begin{equation}\label{eq:M8}
  |M_{8}|
  \leq C\bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\Delta_{h}e_{\psi}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{2}^{2}(\tau^{2}+h^{2})\bigl\|\Delta_{h}\tilde{c}_{1h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
     + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
\end{equation}
In view of Lemma~\ref{lem:b2-bounds}, we deduce
\begin{align}
  \sum_{i=9}^{11}|M_{i}|
  &\leq \Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla(u^{j+1}-u^{j})\bigr\|_{0}
             + \bigl\|\nabla(u^{j} - S_{h}u^{j})\bigr\|_{0}
             + \bigl\|\nabla e_{u}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{2}
    \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}C_{5}^{2}(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
     + CC_{0}^{2}\tau^{2}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\nabla u_{t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t
     + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:M9-11}\\[4pt]
  \sum_{i=12}^{13}|M_{i}|
  &\leq C\bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
    \Bigl(\bigl\|\nabla(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
         + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}h^{2}
     + C\bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
       \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
     + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:M12-13}
\end{align}
The term $M_{7}$ can be split as
\begin{align}
  M_{7}
  &= \Bigl(\bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}
                + (R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})
                + (\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1})\bigr)
           \nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
           \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
               \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr),
  \label{eq:M7-split}
\end{align}
and note that
\begin{align}
  \Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
        \nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr)
  &= D_{\tau}\Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
                   \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad - \Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla D_{\tau}(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
               \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad - \Bigl(D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla(\psi^{j} - R_{h}\psi^{j}),\,
               \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr),
  \label{eq:M7-tech}
\end{align}
thus, we obtain
\begin{align}
  |M_{7}|
  &\leq \Bigl(\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,6}
             + \bigl\|R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,6}
             + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,6}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0,3}
    \bigl\|\nabla D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
    \bigl\|\nabla(D_{\tau}\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}D_{\tau}\psi^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
    \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \bigl\|D_{\tau}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
    \bigl\|\nabla(\psi^{j} - R_{h}\psi^{j})\bigr\|_{0}
    \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + D_{\tau}\Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
                   \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{2}\!\left(\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
    + C_{0}^{2}h^{2}
    + \tau\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}\|\nabla\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{0}^{2}\,\mathrm{d}t\right)
   + \frac{1}{10}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{\phantom{j+1}\!}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \frac{CC_{0}^{2}h^{2}}{\tau}\int_{t_{j}}^{t_{j+1}}
    \bigl(\|\psi_{t}\|_{2}^{2} + \|\tilde{c}_{1t}\|_{2}^{2}\bigr)\,\mathrm{d}t
   + C\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + D_{\tau}\Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{j+1}\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
                   \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr).
  \label{eq:M7}
\end{align}
Inserting \eqref{eq:M1M2}--\eqref{eq:M7} into \eqref{eq:M-sum}, then summing up from
$j = 0$ to $J-1$, we deduce
\begin{align}
  &\tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq C(C_{2}^{2}C_{4}^{2} + C_{0}^{2}C_{5}^{2})(\tau^{2}+h^{2})
   + C\tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\Bigl(C_{0}^{2}
                                 + \bigl\|A_{h}u_{h}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}\Bigr)
    \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
   + \Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{J}\nabla(\psi^{J} - R_{h}\psi^{J}),\,
           \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\Bigr).
  \label{eq:H1-c1-pre}
\end{align}
In addition, observing that
\begin{align}
  \Bigl|\Bigl(\tilde{c}_{1}^{J}\nabla(\psi^{J} - R_{h}\psi^{J}),\,
              \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\Bigr)\Bigr|
  &\leq \bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{J}\bigr\|_{0,\infty}
         \bigl\|\nabla(\psi^{J} - R_{h}\psi^{J})\bigr\|_{0}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq CC_{0}^{4}h^{2} + \frac{1}{2}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2},
  \label{eq:boundary-term}
\end{align}
then substituting \eqref{eq:boundary-term} into \eqref{eq:H1-c1-pre}, and using the Gronwall
lemma, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:H1-c1-final}
  \tau\sum_{j=0}^{J-1}\bigl\|D_{\tau}e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  + \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{J}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
  \leq C(C_{2}^{2}C_{4}^{2} + C_{0}^{2}C_{5}^{2})
       \exp\!\bigl(CC_{0}^{2} + CC_{4}^{2}\bigr)(\tau^{2}+h^{2}).
\end{equation}
A similar estimate can be obtained for $e_{\tilde{c}_{2}}^{j+1}$. The proof is completed.
\end{proof}

Finally, based on Theorems~\ref{thm:l2}--\ref{thm:H1-c}, and using the triangle inequality,
we have

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:H1-all}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we have
\begin{align}
  &\bigl\|c_{1}^{j+1} - c_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|c_{2}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|u^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - \psi_{h}^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
   \leq C_{2}(\tau + h),
  \label{eq:final-L2}\\[4pt]
  &\bigl\|\nabla(u^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
   \leq C_{5}(\tau + h),
  \label{eq:final-H1-u}\\[4pt]
  &\bigl\|\nabla(c_{1}^{j+1} - c_{1h}^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
   + \bigl\|\nabla(c_{2}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0}
   \leq C_{6}(\tau + h).
  \label{eq:final-H1-c}
\end{align}
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Improved error estimate for the
  \texorpdfstring{$\ell^{\infty}(L^{2})$}{l-inf(L2)}-norm}
% ------------------------------------------------------------

In this section, let $\Omega$ be a rectangular domain with the boundary $\partial\Omega$
parallel to $x_{1}$-axis or $x_{2}$-axis. $\mathcal{T}_{h}$ is a family of axis-parallel
rectangular meshes of $\Omega$, and $K$ is a rectangular element. We use the bilinear element
to solve $c_{1}$, $c_{2}$, $\psi$, and $Q_{1}$-$Q_{0}$ element for $u$ and $p$
\cite{John2016}.

Then, we have the following improved estimate.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:improved-l2}
For all $\psi\in H^{3}(\Omega)$, $\varphi_{h}\in X_{h}^{0}$, there holds
\begin{equation}\label{eq:improved-estimate}
  \bigl(\nabla(\psi - R_{h}\psi),\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
  \leq Ch^{2}\|\psi\|_{3}\|\nabla\varphi_{h}\|_{0}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $I_{h}$ be the interpolation operator on $X_{h}^{0}$, then we obtain from
\cite{Lin2006} that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Ih-estimate}
  \bigl(\nabla(\psi - I_{h}\psi),\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
  \leq Ch^{2}\|\psi\|_{3}\|\nabla\varphi_{h}\|_{0}.
\end{equation}
By use of \eqref{eq:Ritz-def}, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Ritz-Ih}
  \bigl(\nabla(R_{h}\psi - I_{h}\psi),\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
  = \bigl(\nabla(\psi - I_{h}\psi),\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr),
  \quad \forall\,\varphi_{h}\in X_{h}.
\end{equation}
Taking $\varphi_{h} = R_{h}\psi - I_{h}\psi$ in \eqref{eq:Ritz-Ih} and using
\eqref{eq:Ih-estimate}, we infer
\begin{equation}\label{eq:Ritz-Ih-bound}
  \bigl\|\nabla(R_{h}\psi - I_{h}\psi)\bigr\|_{0} \leq Ch^{2}\|\psi\|_{3}.
\end{equation}
As a result, from \eqref{eq:Ih-estimate} and \eqref{eq:Ritz-Ih-bound}, we deduce
\begin{align}
  \bigl(\nabla(\psi - R_{h}\psi),\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
  &= \bigl(\nabla(\psi - I_{h}\psi),\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
   + \bigl(\nabla(I_{h}\psi - R_{h}\psi),\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\bigr)
  \leq Ch^{2}\|\psi\|_{3}\|\nabla\varphi_{h}\|_{0}.
  \label{eq:improved-proof}
\end{align}
The proof is completed.
\end{proof}

Based on Lemma~\ref{lem:improved-l2}, we can improve the estimate of $E_{7}$ in
Lemma~\ref{lem:ec-l2}. In fact, $E_{7}$ can be split as
\begin{align}
  E_{7}
  &= \Bigl(\bigl(e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}
                + (R_{h}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - \tilde{c}_{1}^{j})
                + (\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} - P_{0}^{K}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j})\bigr)
           \nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
           \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad + \sum_{K} P_{0}^{K}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}
    \Bigl(\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - \tilde{R}_{h}\psi^{j+1}),\,
          \nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\Bigr),
  \label{eq:E7-split}
\end{align}
where $P_{0}^{K}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j} = \dfrac{1}{|K|}\displaystyle\int_{K}\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\,\mathrm{d}x$.

Then, using Lemma~\ref{lem:improved-l2}, we infer
\begin{align}
  |E_{7}|
  &\leq C\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0,3}
         \bigl\|\nabla(\psi^{j+1} - R_{h}\psi^{j+1})\bigr\|_{0,6}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
       + Ch^{2}\bigl\|\psi^{j+1}\bigr\|_{3}\bigl\|\tilde{c}_{1}^{j}\bigr\|_{2}
         \bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq CC_{0}^{4}h^{4}
     + CC_{0}^{2}\bigl\|e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}
     + \frac{1}{7}\bigl\|\nabla e_{\tilde{c}_{1}}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:E7-improved}
\end{align}
Consequently, we can improve the result of Theorem~\ref{thm:l2} as follows.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm:improved-l2}
Under the conditions of Lemma~\ref{lem:epsi-l2}, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq:improved-l2-result}
  \bigl\|c_{1}^{j+1} - c_{1h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|c_{2}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  + \bigl\|u^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j+1}\bigr\|_{0}
  \leq C_{2}\bigl(\tau + h^{2}\bigr).
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}\label{rem:higher-order}
Although only the first order spatial accuracy is reported in Theorem~\ref{thm:H1-all},
a higher order finite element can also be applied, and higher order error estimates can be
obtained under the condition of a higher regularity of solutions. In addition, on rectangular
meshes, there has been some existing numerical work about the super-convergence analysis of
the PNP equations and the Navier-Stokes equations \cite{Shi2019,Lin2006}. In a similar way,
we can obtain the super-convergence results for the NSPNP equations, and the convergence
order of the $\ell^{\infty}(H^{1})$-norm can be improved to $\mathcal{O}(\tau + h^{2})$.
\end{remark}

\section{Two physical properties}
\label{sec:physical}
% ----------------------------------------------------------

In this section, we aim to derive some important physical properties for the proposed scheme.

\begin{theorem}[Global mass conservation]
\label{thm:mass}
The finite element solutions $\bigl(c_{1h}^{j+1},\, c_{2h}^{j+1}\bigr)$ satisfy global mass
conservation, that is,
\begin{equation}
  \int_{\Omega} c_{1h}^{j+1}\,\mathrm{d}x
  \;=\;
  \int_{\Omega} c_{2h}^{j+1}\,\mathrm{d}x
  \;=\;
  m_{0}\,|\Omega|.
  \label{eq:mass-cons}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

The property \eqref{eq:mass-cons} can be derived easily from \eqref{eq:step2}.

\begin{theorem}[Energy stability]
\label{thm:energy}
Define a discrete energy by
\begin{equation}
  E\!\left(u_{h}^{j},\,\psi_{h}^{j}\right)
  \;=\;
  \frac{1}{2}\left(
    \left\|u_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    +
    \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \right).
  \label{eq:energy-def}
\end{equation}
Then, if $\tau$ and $h$ are small enough, an energy stability
\begin{equation}
  E\!\left(u_{h}^{j+1},\,\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  \;\leq\;
  E\!\left(u_{h}^{j},\,\psi_{h}^{j}\right)
  \label{eq:energy-stab}
\end{equation}
holds for $j = 0, 1, \ldots, J-1$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Step 1.}
Choose $\varphi_{h} = \Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}$ and
$\varphi_{h} = c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}$ in \eqref{eq:scheme} respectively, and we get
\begin{align}
  \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  &= \left(\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, \nabla\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
   = \left(c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1},\, \Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
   \nonumber\\
  &\leq
   \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}
   \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0},
   \label{eq:step1a}\\[4pt]
  \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  &= \left(\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, \nabla(c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1})\right)
   = \left(c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1},\, \Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
   \nonumber\\
  &\leq
   \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}
   \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0},
   \label{eq:step1b}
\end{align}
which implies
\begin{align}
  \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}
  &= \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0},
  \label{eq:Deltapsi-eq}\\
  \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  &= \left(\nabla(c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}),\,\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right).
  \label{eq:Deltapsi-sq}
\end{align}

\textit{Step 2.}
Putting $v_{h} = u_{h}^{j+1}$ in \eqref{eq:scheme} yields
\begin{equation}
  \frac{1}{2\tau}\!\left(
    \left\|u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|u_{h}^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    - \left\|u_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \right)
  + \left\|\nabla u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  = -\!\left((c_{1h}^{j} - c_{2h}^{j})\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, u_{h}^{j+1}\right).
  \label{eq:step2-u}
\end{equation}

\textit{Step 3.}
From the last equation of \eqref{eq:scheme}, it is easy to find
\begin{equation}
  \left(\nabla D_{\tau}\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, \nabla\varphi_{h}\right)
  = \left(D_{\tau}(c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}),\, \varphi_{h}\right),
  \quad \forall\,\varphi_{h} \in X_{h}.
  \label{eq:step3-psi}
\end{equation}
Take $\eta_{1h} = \eta_{2h} = \psi_{h}^{j+1}$ in \eqref{eq:scheme} and
$\varphi_{h} = \psi_{h}^{j+1}$ in \eqref{eq:step3-psi}, respectively; then we arrive at
\begin{align}
  &\frac{1}{2\tau}\!\left(
    \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|\nabla(\psi_{h}^{j+1} - \psi_{h}^{j})\right\|_{0}^{2}
    - \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \right)
  + \left(\nabla(c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}),\, \nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  + \left((c_{1h}^{j} + c_{2h}^{j})\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, \nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  = -b_{2}\!\left(u_{h}^{j};\, c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1},\, \psi_{h}^{j+1}\right).
  \label{eq:step3-sum}
\end{align}

\textit{Step 4.}
Adding \eqref{eq:step2-u} and \eqref{eq:step3-sum} and using \eqref{eq:Deltapsi-sq}, we deduce
\begin{align}
  &\frac{1}{2\tau}\!\Bigl(
    \left\|u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|u_{h}^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    - \left\|u_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|\nabla(\psi_{h}^{j+1} - \psi_{h}^{j})\right\|_{0}^{2}
    - \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \Bigr)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  + \left\|\nabla u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  + \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  + \left\|\sqrt{c_{1}^{j} + c_{2}^{j}}\,\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &= -\!\left((c_{1h}^{j} - c_{1}^{j} + c_{2h}^{j} - c_{2}^{j})\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\,
              \nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  + \left((c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{1h}^{j} - (c_{2h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j}))\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\,
         u_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  - \left((c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1})\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1},\, u_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  + b_{2}\!\left(u_{h}^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j};\, c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1},\, \psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  - b_{2}\!\left(u_{h}^{j+1};\, c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1},\, \psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)
  \nonumber\\
  &=: \sum_{i=1}^{5} R_{i}.
  \label{eq:step4-Ri}
\end{align}

Next, we estimate the above five terms. By use of Theorem~\ref{thm:L2error}, it is easy to check
\begin{equation}
  \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{1h}^{j}\right\|_{0}
  \leq
  \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{1}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}
  + \left\|c_{1h}^{j} - c_{1}^{j}\right\|_{0}
  + \left\|c_{1}^{j+1} - c_{1}^{j}\right\|_{0}
  \leq C_{2}(\tau + h).
  \label{eq:c1-diff}
\end{equation}
Similarly, we obtain
\begin{equation}
  \left\|c_{2h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j}\right\|_{0}
  \leq C_{2}(\tau + h),
  \qquad
  \left\|\nabla\!\left(u_{h}^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j}\right)\right\|_{0}
  \leq C_{5}(\tau + h).
  \label{eq:u-diff}
\end{equation}
Then, based on \eqref{eq:Deltapsi-eq}, \eqref{eq:c1-diff}, \eqref{eq:u-diff} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:L2error}, we infer
\begin{align}
  |R_{1}| + |R_{2}| + |R_{4}|
  &\leq
  \left(\left\|c_{1h}^{j} - c_{1}^{j}\right\|_{0}
        + \left\|c_{2h}^{j} - c_{2}^{j}\right\|_{0}\right)
  \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0,3}
  \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0,6}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  + \left(\left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{1h}^{j}\right\|_{0}
          + \left\|c_{2h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j}\right\|_{0}\right)
  \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0,3}
  \left\|u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0,6}
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  + \left\|\nabla\!\left(u_{h}^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j}\right)\right\|_{0}
  \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}
  \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq
  C_{5}(\tau + h)\left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  + \frac{1}{2}\left\|\nabla u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:R1R2R4}
\end{align}
Using Green's formula, Lemma~\ref{lem:1} (equation \eqref{eq:Lem1}), Theorem~\ref{thm:L2error}
and \eqref{eq:Deltapsi-eq}, we get
\begin{align}
  |R_{3} + R_{5}|
  &= \frac{1}{2}
     \left|\left(\mathrm{div}\,u_{h}^{j+1},\,
                 (c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1})\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)\right|
  \nonumber\\
  &= \frac{1}{2}
     \left|\left(\mathrm{div}(u_{h}^{j+1} - u^{j+1}),\,
                 (c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1})\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right)\right|
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq
  C_{5}(\tau + h)
  \left\|c_{1h}^{j+1} - c_{2h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}
  \left\|\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0,\infty}
  \leq
  C_{5}(\tau + h)\left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}.
  \label{eq:R3R5}
\end{align}
Inserting \eqref{eq:R1R2R4} and \eqref{eq:R3R5} into \eqref{eq:step4-Ri} yields
\begin{align}
  &\frac{1}{2}\!\left(
    \left\|u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \right)
  + \frac{1}{2}\!\left(
    \left\|u_{h}^{j+1} - u_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|\nabla(\psi_{h}^{j+1} - \psi_{h}^{j})\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \right)
  \nonumber\\
  &\quad
  + \tau\!\left(1 - C_{5}(\tau + h)\right)
    \left\|\Delta_{h}\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  + \frac{1}{2}\,\tau\left\|\nabla u_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  + \tau\left\|\sqrt{c_{1}^{j} + c_{2}^{j}}\,\nabla\psi_{h}^{j+1}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \nonumber\\
  &\leq
  \frac{1}{2}\!\left(
    \left\|u_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
    + \left\|\nabla\psi_{h}^{j}\right\|_{0}^{2}
  \right).
  \label{eq:energy-ineq}
\end{align}
If $\tau$ and $h$ are small enough, \eqref{eq:energy-stab} holds.
\end{proof}

\section{Conclusion and future work}
\label{sec:conclusion}
% ----------------------------------------------------------

In this work, we present a linearized backward Euler finite element scheme for the NSPNP
equations, and prove the existence and uniqueness of this scheme. We then obtain the
unconditional error estimates of all variables in $l^{\infty}(L^{2})$ and
$l^{\infty}(H^{1})$ norms. Moreover, we give two important physical properties: the global
mass conservation and energy stability.

However, the concentration positivity cannot be theoretically justified, which is also
important for the numerical scheme of the NSPNP equations. In fact, for the pure PNP
equations, there has been some existing numerical methods which keep these physical
properties. In the future, we will try to apply these methods to the NSPNP equations. In
addition, our numerical scheme is only first-order accurate in time, and in a forthcoming
research we plan to propose some second-order accurate numerical schemes for the NSPNP
equations, such as the Crank--Nicolson scheme and the BDF2 scheme. Finally, it is
interesting and challenging to construct robust schemes to solve some practical problems,
such as charging dynamics in a nanopore, electroosmotic flow in a nanochannel, and
electroconvective flow on an ion-selective surface \cite{Wang22017}, and so on.
